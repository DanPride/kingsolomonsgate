Courtney's Courtly Love, Cobain's Dance

Soho District,
New York City
1990's


Courtney Loves band was playing Bunrattys for three days. As I entered the bar the first day She walked up to me and introduced herself. "Hi I'm Courtney... Courtney Love" she said suggestively cupping her hand toward her breast as she said it. I immediately replied "Is that anything like Courtley Love?"

I was immediately smitten and spent much of the next three days with her flirting and trying my best. and learning of the problems a broken jaw can present one with. Contrary to the publicity she recieved much later, the Courtney I knew was a sweet and dear person. Witty, fun and very sexy. But then she was gone on her way, three nights in the thousand nights I spent in the Boston Heavy Metal scene.

The Scene itself also came and went as did I. I Eventually arrived in New York City to work as a programmer for RCA Records. Altho I had nothing to do with "A+R" (getting people signed to a record deal), I found that those three letters on the Business Card and the thousand dollar Brooks Brother suits I was fond of wearing in those days,... did remarkable things for my New York night life.

So it was that on an unusually self abusive night I wound up leaning against a wall in some loft in Soho while the new talent at RCA strutted its stuff for the industry insiders.

Leaning against the wall in I must admitt a near stupor, this singer came walking down along the line of about thirty industry execs, pausing as he passed me. He Sang right at me to be noticed, I stirred, I looked up,... and managed an unsophisticated "Hey that's pretty good".

Cobain's face brightened in a wide smile and with a nod toward me he continued on down the line singing teen spirt as he went.

The song of course was soon everywhere and then I knew who he was.

I listened and grew to appreciate his music and would occasionally catch glimpses of him in this or that media. I noted his suicide with a bit of disinterest until I suddenly became aware that Courtney had become his wife.

It suddenly became personal. My attitude changed abruptly toward him.

How could he be such an SOB as to do that to her?

Its the part of life the suiciders never see
